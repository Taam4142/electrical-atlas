# Master Subject Map

This is the canonical subject backbone. It gives every topic one navigational home while the taxonomy graph records other valid contexts.

## 01 — Foundations and supporting science

### 01.01 Mathematics

- Arithmetic, ratios, proportional reasoning, scientific notation, significant figures, uncertainty
- Algebra, functions, graphs, logarithms, exponentials, complex numbers, phasors
- Geometry, trigonometry, coordinate systems, vectors, tensors
- Differential and integral calculus, vector calculus, differential equations
- Linear algebra, transforms, eigenanalysis, state spaces
- Probability, random processes, statistics, estimation, information theory
- Discrete mathematics, Boolean algebra, graph theory, number theory
- Numerical methods, optimization, convexity, computational complexity

### 01.02 Physics

- Mechanics, oscillation, waves, thermodynamics, statistical mechanics
- Classical electromagnetism, relativity, quantum mechanics, solid-state physics
- Optics, acoustics, plasma physics, radiation, nuclear physics
- Measurement, dimensional analysis, physical constants, model validity

### 01.03 Chemistry and materials science

- Atomic bonding, crystal structure, defects, phase diagrams, diffusion
- Electrochemistry, corrosion, polymers, ceramics, metals, composites
- Electronic, magnetic, dielectric, optical, thermal, and mechanical properties
- Surface science, thin films, nanomaterials, processing, characterization

### 01.04 Computation

- Algorithms, data structures, programming paradigms, numerical computing
- Computer systems, operating systems, networking, databases, security
- Scientific computing, simulation, data analysis, machine learning

## 02 — Electricity, magnetism, and electromagnetic foundations

### 02.01 Charge and matter

- Elementary charge, charge conservation, quantization, distributions
- Electrons, ions, holes, charged particles, quasiparticles
- Conductors, insulators, semiconductors, electrolytes, plasmas

### 02.02 Electrostatics and potential

- Coulomb force, electric field, flux, Gauss's law
- Potential energy, electric potential, voltage, capacitance
- Polarization, dielectric response, boundary conditions, breakdown

### 02.03 Current and transport

- Current density, continuity equation, drift, mobility, diffusion
- Conductivity, resistivity, Ohm's law, Joule heating
- Ballistic, diffusive, ionic, superconducting, and vacuum transport

### 02.04 Magnetism and induction

- Magnetic field, force, flux, magnetic materials, hysteresis
- Biot–Savart, Ampère–Maxwell, Faraday–Lenz, motional EMF
- Inductance, mutual coupling, magnetic circuits, eddy currents

### 02.05 Electrodynamics

- Maxwell's equations, potentials, gauges, waves, radiation
- Energy and momentum, Poynting theorem, stress tensor
- Quasistatic approximations, lumped/distributed boundaries, relativity

## 03 — Circuit theory, network analysis, and simulation

### 03.01 Circuit quantities and ideal elements

- Voltage/current reference directions, power sign convention

- Energy storage, passivity, linearity, time invariance, causality

### 03.02 Analysis methods

- Kirchhoff laws, nodal, mesh, superposition, source transformation
- Thévenin/Norton, maximum power transfer, reciprocity, network theorems
- First/second-order transients, natural/forced response, initial conditions
- Sinusoidal steady state, impedance, resonance, power, three-phase circuits
- Laplace/Fourier methods, transfer functions, poles/zeros, state space

### 03.03 Networks and synthesis

- Two-port and multiport networks, scattering parameters, immittance matrices
- Passive network synthesis, filters, matching, stability, sensitivity
- Graph/topological methods, nonlinear circuits, switched circuits

### 03.04 Simulation

- SPICE models, DC/AC/transient/noise analyses, convergence
- Behavioral modeling, Monte Carlo, worst-case, sensitivity
- Electromagnetic, thermal, multiphysics, hardware-in-loop, model validation
